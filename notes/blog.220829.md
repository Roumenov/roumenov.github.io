Yesterday I had a nice chat with a buddy about making, "content". There's that awful tension between editing to make sure you're making the **right** thing in the right _way_, and making enough of **anything** to put it _anywhere_.

## Scope

Every tradition of work has some kind of addage about not biting off more than you can chew. With the goal of making sure you at least have something to put somewhere. Engineers used to say K.I.S.S(keep it simple, stupid), with the startup boom everyone got to know MVP. My favorite addage is an art teacher of ours who would tell students to stop _masturbating_ over their work. I always loved the layer of vulgarity to that.

A funny inverse to this is the tendency toward laziness. Everything sounds good when you're imagining it done, but making it happen is _way less fun_.

After we've finished whatever we're all up to, we look at the sum total or a generation of work and notice that everything looks sooo.... **similar**. It's very stark when you have a professional perspective and know the source material. You really _can_ tell when someone looked at the cliff notes.

## The Chatter

It leads to this other phenomenon with trends, where people will take the accumulated avg of everything "we"(whatever community) are [chattering](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/belief/2009/jul/20/heidegger-being-time-critchley#:~:text=Conscience%20calls%20Dasein,back%20to%20myself.) about and treat it like that avg represents the location of the next peak that everyone will want to climb.

We hear something and want to treat it like a conclusive prediction. Or at least a committed statement, something that represents _someone's_ beliefs. Probably because predictions are fun! But the fun carries us away from the intellectual rigor and **work** of actually setting out a plan and carving out our minimum viable product.
